## Description

Adversaries may compromise third-party servers that can be used during targeting. Use of servers allows an adversary to stage, launch, and execute an operation. During post-compromise activity, adversaries may utilize servers for various tasks, including for Command and Control.[^2]  Instead of purchasing a [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1583.004-Server\|Server]] or [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1583.003-Virtual_Private_Server\|Virtual Private Server]], adversaries may compromise third-party servers in support of operations.<br><br>Adversaries may also compromise web servers to support watering hole operations, as in [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1189-Drive-by_Compromise\|Drive-by Compromise]], or email servers to support [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1566-Phishing\|Phishing]] operations.
