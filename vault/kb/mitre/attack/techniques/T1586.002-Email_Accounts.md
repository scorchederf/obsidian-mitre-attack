## Description

Adversaries may compromise email accounts that can be used during targeting. Adversaries can use compromised email accounts to further their operations, such as leveraging them to conduct [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1598-Phishing_for_Information\|Phishing for Information]], [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1566-Phishing\|Phishing]], or large-scale spam email campaigns. Utilizing an existing persona with a compromised email account may engender a level of trust in a potential victim if they have a relationship with, or knowledge of, the compromised persona. Compromised email accounts can also be used in the acquisition of infrastructure (ex: [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1583.001-Domains\|Domains]]).<br><br>A variety of methods exist for compromising email accounts, such as gathering credentials via [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1598-Phishing_for_Information\|Phishing for Information]], purchasing credentials from third-party sites, brute forcing credentials (ex: password reuse from breach credential dumps), or paying employees, suppliers or business partners for access to credentials.[^1] [^2]  Prior to compromising email accounts, adversaries may conduct Reconnaissance to inform decisions about which accounts to compromise to further their operation. Adversaries may target compromising well-known email accounts or domains from which malicious spam or [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1566-Phishing\|Phishing]] emails may evade reputation-based email filtering rules.<br><br>Adversaries can use a compromised email account to hijack existing email threads with targets of interest.
