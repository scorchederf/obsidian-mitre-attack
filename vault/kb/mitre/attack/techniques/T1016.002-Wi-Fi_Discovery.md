## Description

Adversaries may search for information about Wi-Fi networks, such as network names and passwords, on compromised systems. Adversaries may use Wi-Fi information as part of [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1087-Account_Discovery\|Account Discovery]], [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1018-Remote_System_Discovery\|Remote System Discovery]], and other discovery or [[kb/mitre/attack/tactics/TA0006-Credential_Access\|Credential Access]] activity to support both ongoing and future campaigns.<br><br>Adversaries may collect various types of information about Wi-Fi networks from hosts. For example, on Windows names and passwords of all Wi-Fi networks a device has previously connected to may be available through `netsh wlan show profiles` to enumerate Wi-Fi names and then `netsh wlan show profile “Wi-Fi name” key=clear` to show a Wi-Fi network’s corresponding password.[^4] [^5] [^6]  Additionally, names and other details of locally reachable Wi-Fi networks can be discovered using calls to `wlanAPI.dll` [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1106-Native_API\|Native API]] functions.[^3] <br><br>On Linux, names and passwords of all Wi-Fi-networks a device has previously connected to may be available in files under ` /etc/NetworkManager/system-connections/`.[^2]  On macOS, the password of a known Wi-Fi may be identified with ` security find-generic-password -wa wifiname` (requires admin username/password).[^1] <br>
