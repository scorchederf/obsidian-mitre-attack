## Description

Adversaries may abuse launchctl to execute commands or programs. Launchctl interfaces with launchd, the service management framework for macOS. Launchctl supports taking subcommands on the command-line, interactively, or even redirected from standard input.[^1] <br><br>Adversaries use launchctl to execute commands and programs as [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1543.001-Launch_Agent\|Launch Agent]]s or [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1543.004-Launch_Daemon\|Launch Daemon]]s. Common subcommands include: `launchctl load`,`launchctl unload`, and `launchctl start`. Adversaries can use scripts or manually run the commands `launchctl load -w "%s/Library/LaunchAgents/%s"` or `/bin/launchctl load` to execute [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1543.001-Launch_Agent\|Launch Agent]]s or [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1543.004-Launch_Daemon\|Launch Daemon]]s.[^3] [^2] <br>
