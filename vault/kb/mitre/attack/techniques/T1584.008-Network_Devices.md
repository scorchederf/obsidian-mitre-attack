## Description

Adversaries may compromise third-party network devices that can be used during targeting. Network devices, such as small office/home office (SOHO) routers, may be compromised where the adversary's ultimate goal is not [[kb/mitre/attack/tactics/TA0001-Initial_Access\|Initial Access]] to that environment, but rather to leverage these devices to support additional targeting.<br><br>Once an adversary has control, compromised network devices can be used to launch additional operations, such as hosting payloads for [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1566-Phishing\|Phishing]] campaigns (i.e., [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1608.005-Link_Target\|Link Target]]) or enabling the required access to execute [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1659-Content_Injection\|Content Injection]] operations. Adversaries may also be able to harvest reusable credentials (i.e., [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1078-Valid_Accounts\|Valid Accounts]]) from compromised network devices.<br><br>Adversaries often target Internet-facing edge devices and related network appliances that specifically do not support robust host-based defenses.[^3] [^1] <br><br>Compromised network devices may be used to support subsequent [[kb/mitre/attack/tactics/TA0011-Command_and_Control\|Command and Control]] activity, such as [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1665-Hide_Infrastructure\|Hide Infrastructure]] through an established [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1090-Proxy\|Proxy]] and/or [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1584.005-Botnet\|Botnet]] network.[^2]
