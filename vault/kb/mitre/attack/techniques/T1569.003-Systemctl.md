## Description

Adversaries may abuse systemctl to execute commands or programs. Systemctl is the primary interface for systemd, the Linux init system and service manager. Typically invoked from a shell, Systemctl can also be integrated into scripts or applications.   <br><br>Adversaries may use systemctl to execute commands or programs as [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1543.002-Systemd_Service\|Systemd Service]]s. Common subcommands include: `systemctl start`, `systemctl stop`, `systemctl enable`, `systemctl disable`, and `systemctl status`.[^1]
