## Description

Adversaries may match or approximate the names of legitimate accounts to make newly created ones appear benign. This will typically occur during [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1136-Create_Account\|Create Account]], although accounts may also be renamed at a later date. This may also coincide with [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1531-Account_Access_Removal\|Account Access Removal]] if the actor first deletes an account before re-creating one with the same name.[^4] <br><br>Often, adversaries will attempt to masquerade as service accounts, such as those associated with legitimate software, data backups, or container cluster management.[^1] [^2]  They may also give accounts generic, trustworthy names, such as “admin”, “help”, or “root.”[^3]  Sometimes adversaries may model account names off of those already existing in the system, as a follow-on behavior to [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1087-Account_Discovery\|Account Discovery]].  <br><br>Note that this is distinct from [[kb/mitre/attack/techniques/T1684.001-Impersonation\|Impersonation]], which describes impersonating specific trusted individuals or organizations, rather than user or service account names.
